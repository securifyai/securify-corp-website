2. Tracking Technologies

When you access our Services, we use cookies, pixels, beacons, local storage and similar technologies ("Tracking Technologies”) to automatically collect information about you, your device, and your online behavior for the purposes of enhancing navigation, improving performance, performing analytics, and customizing your experience.

2.1. Types of Tracking Technologies When you use or access our Services, we use the following categories of Tracking Technologies: (a) Strictly necessary Tracking Technologies: these Tracking Technologies are automatically placed on your computer or device when you access or take certain actions on our Services. These Tracking Technologies are essential to enable you to navigate around and use the features of our Services; (b) Functionality Tracking Technologies: these Tracking Technologies allow our Services to remember choices you make (such as your language) and provide enhanced and personalized features. For example, these Tracking Technologies are used for authentication (to remember when you are logged-in) and support other features of our Services; (c) Performance Tracking Technologies: these Tracking Technologies collect information about your online activity (for example the duration of your visit on our Services), including referral source and IP-based geolocation. These Tracking Technologies are used for analytics, research and to perform statistics (based on aggregated information); and (d) Tracking and Advertising Tracking Technologies: These Tracking Technologies collect information about your browsing habits to limit ad frequency and measure campaign effectiveness. Third-party networks may also use similar technologies, governed by their own privacy policies, which Securify does not control.

2.2. Storage of Tracking Technologies We store “First Party Tracking Technologies” on your device when you access our Services. Third parties (e.g., analytics providers, business partners, advertisers) also store “Third-party Tracking Technologies” on our Services. Both types may persist for a single visit or for repeat visits. You can manage Tracking Technologies via our cookie settings tool (where available) or your browser settings, including sending a “Do-Not-Track” signal. Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of the Services. Below is a list of useful links that can provide you with more information on how to manage your cookies: (a) Google Chrome; (b) Mozilla Firefox; (c) Safari (Desktop); (d) Safari (Mobile); (e) Android Browser; and (f) Microsoft Edge. You can learn more and turn off certain third-party targeting and advertising cookies by visiting the following third-party webpages: (a) The Interactive Advertising Bureau (US); (b) The Interactive Advertising Bureau (EU); (c) European Interactive Digital Advertising Alliance (EU); (d) Digital Advertising Alliance; (e) Network Advertising Initiative.
